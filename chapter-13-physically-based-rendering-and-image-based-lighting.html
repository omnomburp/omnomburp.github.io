Chapter 13

A material looks believable when light, view, and environment agree on the same story

Physically based rendering is not magic realism. It is mostly discipline. Metals should reflect and tint differently from dielectrics. Rough surfaces should spread highlights instead of keeping them razor sharp. The surrounding world should matter because real objects do not only respond to one hand-placed light. Image-based lighting is one way to give that surrounding world a concrete role.

Metalness

Controls whether the surface behaves more like a colored conductor or a dielectric with a small neutral F0.

Roughness

Widens and softens the specular response instead of acting like a vague “shininess” number.

Environment light

Lets reflections and ambient response depend on the world around the object instead of only on one direct light.

Metallic-roughness shortcut F0 = mix(vec3(0.04), baseColor, metalness)
Roughness idea higher roughness = wider lobe
Environment idea reflection samples the world, not empty space

Mental model

Modern material response is structured, not arbitrary

1

Diffuse and specular are not independent decorations

They are two ways light leaves a surface, and good material models keep them in a more coherent relationship.

2

Metals tint reflections strongly

Dielectrics keep a small neutral base reflectance. Metals push more of the base color into the reflective response.

3

Roughness changes shape, not just brightness

A rough highlight should broaden and blur. It is not enough to merely turn the highlight down.

4

Environment lighting stabilizes the look

As the camera moves, the material still feels grounded because the surrounding world keeps contributing light and reflections.

Material story

PBR is mostly about using fewer arbitrary cheats

  1. Start with a base color that means something

    For dielectrics it mostly describes diffuse appearance. For metals it also drives the reflected tint more strongly.

  2. Let Fresnel increase reflection toward grazing angles

    Even simple models benefit when edges feel more reflective than head-on views.

  3. Use roughness to reshape the highlight and reflection

    Low roughness keeps a concentrated lobe. High roughness spreads that energy over a wider region.

  4. Light the object with a world, not just a lamp

    Image-based lighting gives the material something believable to reflect and picks up broad ambient cues from the scene.

Why this helps artists

Material controls become more transferable across scenes because the knobs map to more stable physical ideas.

Common failure mode

A surface has bright specular response and strong diffuse color at the same time in a way that feels energy-incoherent.

IBL intuition

The environment is just another light source, but one that comes from many directions and carries scene color with it.

Good debugging move

Turn roughness to the extremes and rotate the environment. If nothing qualitative changes, the shader model is probably too weak.

Live lab

Slide between dielectric and metal while reshaping the environment response

This demo shades a sphere with a simplified physically based response. The goal is not film accuracy. The goal is to feel how roughness broadens the highlight, how metalness tints the reflection, and how the surrounding world changes the material even when the direct light is kept simple.

  • Set roughness low and metalness high. The bright band should tighten and the reflection tint should become stronger.
  • Raise roughness while keeping metalness high. The highlight should spread and the environment reflection should soften.
  • Rotate the environment and watch the same surface feel different because the incoming world light changed.

The sphere is driven by roughness, metalness, Fresnel-like edge response, and a colored environment. The important idea is how those responsibilities stay distinct.
